Lungs

The lungs are the organs in charge of breathing, which is the process through which the body exchanges oxygen and carbon dioxide with its surroundings. The trachea, which divides into the bronchi and bronchioles, is the airway that the lungs use to take in oxygen. These organs terminate in collections of microscopic air sacs known as alveoli, where oxygen is taken up by the bloodstream and carbon dioxide is exhaled. The rib cage and diaphragm protect the lungs, which aids in controlling breathing. The lungs can be affected by respiratory conditions such asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and lung cancer.
